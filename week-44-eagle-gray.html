Week 44 — PNW Watercolor Series

Eagle Gray

Hundreds of eagles line the Skagit and Nooksack, hunched in alders like dark punctuation. Cedar boughs sag under rain-weight. The world has simplified: gray, green, brown. Abundance disguised as austerity.

Primary Trio

Neutral Tint

Sky, water, gravel, all one gray; the color that does triple duty because November has simplified everything down to value

Perylene Green

Cedar bough heavy and dark; the darkest green that still reads as green, holding the line between color and shadow

Transparent Brown Oxide

Eagle mantle and salmon flank; genuinely transparent, letting white paper glow through as catch-light on plumage

Mixes & Ratios

Gravel Bar

2 Neutral Tint : 1 Transparent Brown Oxide : touch Buff Titanium

River stone under overcast — the color of waiting, of things that stay while the water moves.

Neutral Tint
Trans. Brown Oxide
Buff Titanium
Mix on palette to warm mid-gray. Apply in varied washes. Buff Titanium touch prevents the mix from going cold. Leave white paper gaps for water between stones.

Cedar Droop

2 Perylene Green : 1 Burnt Umber

Bough weighted past horizontal — the geometry of rain-load, where gravity becomes visible in the shape of a tree.

Perylene Green
Burnt Umber
Perylene Green’s staining intensity dominates, but Burnt Umber ages it. Apply with loaded brush in downward strokes, heavier at frond tips. The mix should feel like gravity.

Raptor Wire

2 Ivory Black : 1 Neutral Tint

Thirty eagles on a power line, dusk — dark punctuation on a dark sentence, readable only because you know what to look for.

Ivory Black
Neutral Tint
Neutral Tint softens Ivory Black. Paint the wire first as single horizontal line, add eagle shapes while still damp so they bleed slightly into the cable. Dusk means no hard edges.

Why These Colors?

This palette is deliberately austere. Neutral Tint does triple duty as sky, water, and stone. Perylene Green is the darkest green that still reads as green. Transparent Brown Oxide lets white paper glow through as eagle plumage catch-light. Ivory Black provides absolute darks. Cerulean Blue appears only when clouds break. Quinacridone Gold is the single warm note.

Painting Prompts

Gray, green, brown. The world has simplified, and these prompts work with that austerity rather than fighting it — abundance disguised as restraint.

Prompt 1

Eagles on the Wire

Subject: Power line with thirty-plus eagle silhouettes at dusk — dark punctuation marks against a gray sky, the most abundant raptor scene you’ll ever paint.

Raptor Wire (Ivory Black 2:Neutral Tint 1) handles both cable and birds. Buff Titanium on only two or three nearest heads — white used as sparingly as the eagles use motion.

  • Graded Neutral Tint wash for sky
  • Paint wire with rigger brush
  • Add eagle shapes with Raptor Wire mix while damp
  • Touch Buff Titanium to only two or three nearest bird heads
Prompt 2

Gravel Bar with Salmon Carcasses

Subject: River gravel bar scattered with spent chum, a single eagle standing among the dead — the feast that looks like a graveyard.

Gravel Bar (Neutral Tint 2:Transparent Brown Oxide 1:Buff Titanium touch) for the stones. Transparent Brown Oxide for carcasses. Raw Sienna dots for the roe that starts the cycle again.

  • Paint gravel first with Gravel Bar mix
  • Salmon carcasses with Transparent Brown Oxide
  • Eagle is Ivory Black body, Buff Titanium head
  • Raw Sienna dots for salmon roe
Prompt 3

Cedar Boughs Over River

Subject: Rain-heavy cedar drooping over gray river — the boughs sagging past horizontal, geometry rewritten by water weight.

Cedar Droop (Perylene Green 2:Burnt Umber 1) applied in downward strokes, heavier at frond tips. The mix should feel like gravity made visible.

  • Paint Cerulean Blue sky gap first
  • Cedar boughs with Cedar Droop mix in downward strokes
  • River is flat Neutral Tint with cedar reflection charged in
  • Undersea Green for deepest pool areas
Prompt 4

The Cottonwood Leaf in the Eddy

Subject: Single golden leaf spinning in river eddy, surrounded by gray — the smallest subject in the palette, and the one that earns Quinacridone Gold its place.

Quinacridone Gold gets one confident shape. Everything else is gray water. This painting is about restraint paying off in a single warm note.

  • Paint eddy water first: Neutral Tint in circular strokes
  • Let dry completely
  • Paint leaf with pure Quinacridone Gold, one confident shape
  • Add submerged gravel shapes beneath water wash
Notes: All pigments are Daniel Smith extra fine watercolors. Transparent Brown Oxide is one of the most underrated pigments — genuinely transparent, non-granulating, warmer than Raw Umber. Undersea Green is a Daniel Smith convenience mix. Ivory Black is carbon-based and slightly warm compared to Lamp Black.
