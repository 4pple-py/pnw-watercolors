Week 35 — PNW Watercolor Series

Equinox Tilt

Three minutes of light vanish daily — you can feel the planet leaning. Chinook salmon shove upstream, bodies already mottled red-green. Maple gold appears in patches like a quilt being stitched. Bracket fungi shelf out from nurse logs. The equinox doesn’t balance anything; it accelerates the fall.

Primary Trio

Quinacridone Gold

Maple patches catching low sun, the quilt squares of early autumn; semi-transparent and warm, impossible to ignore against a dark canopy

Green Apatite Genuine

Moss-draped old-growth trunk, heavily granulating; a PrimaTek pigment ground from actual mineral, unmatched texture

Indigo

Shadow arriving earlier each day; deepens aggressively with concentration, the encroaching dark of shortening days

Mixes & Ratios

Spawning Mottle

1 Pyrrol Scarlet : 1 Hooker’s Green : 1 Transparent Red Oxide

Chinook flank breaking down into spawning color — mottled, patchy, mid-transformation, a fish arguing with its own body about what color to be.

Pyrrol Scarlet
Hooker's Green
Tr. Red Oxide
Do not pre-mix. Drop all three onto wet paper and let them collide. Spawning salmon are not evenly colored — they’re mottled, patchy, mid-transformation. The result should look like a fish arguing with its own body about what color to be. That’s accurate.

Nurse Shelf

2 Raw Umber : 1 Burnt Sienna : touch Green Apatite Genuine

Bracket fungus on a decomposing nurse log — concentric rings of growth, the slow architecture of decay.

Raw Umber
Burnt Sienna
Green Apatite
Paint concentric arcs, not flat washes. Raw Umber for the older outer rings, Burnt Sienna for the actively growing margin. The Green Apatite Genuine touch represents moss beginning to colonize the fungus itself.

Valley Sink

2 Indigo : 1 Buff Titanium

Fog filling the lowlands as light retreats — milky-blue at medium dilution, the visible breath of shortening days.

Indigo
Buff Titanium
Buff Titanium’s opacity turns Indigo’s transparency into fog — literally. The mix should be milky-blue at medium dilution. Leave the ridge tops untouched paper or dry Quinacridone Gold.

Why These Colors?

The equinox palette is engineered around the concept of unequal forces meeting. Quinacridone Gold appears in patches, not sheets — autumn is arriving piecemeal. Green Apatite Genuine is a PrimaTek pigment ground from actual mineral; its granulation and texture are unmatched for representing the ancient, slow-time surfaces of old-growth bark and moss. Indigo deepens aggressively with concentration. Pyrrol Scarlet and Transparent Red Oxide handle the salmon’s transformation — one opaque and vivid, the other transparent and earthy.

Painting Prompts

Stuck staring at a blank page? These subjects are tailor-made for this week’s palette — each one leans into the colors you already have mixed and the PNW moment happening right outside.

Prompt 1

Chinook in the Shallows

Subject: A mottled chinook salmon in knee-deep water, body breaking down into spawning color — red and green arguing on the same flank.

The Spawning Mottle mix (Pyrrol Scarlet 1:Hooker’s Green 1:Transparent Red Oxide 1) dropped onto wet paper without pre-mixing creates the patchy, mid-transformation skin of a spawning chinook.

  • Do not pre-mix the three pigments — drop them onto wet paper and let them collide
  • Paint the gravel bed first with Raw Umber
  • The hooked jaw gets Transparent Red Oxide
  • Indigo shadows under the fish
Prompt 2

Bracket Fungi on a Nurse Log

Subject: Shelf after shelf of bracket fungi on a moss-covered nurse log — concentric rings of growth, the slow architecture of decay.

The Nurse Shelf mix (Raw Umber 2:Burnt Sienna 1:Green Apatite Genuine touch) paints concentric arcs that mirror the growth rings of the fungus itself.

  • Paint concentric arcs, not flat washes
  • Green Apatite Genuine touch on the oldest shelves where moss is colonizing
  • The nurse log is Raw Umber darkened with Indigo
  • Use cold-press or rough paper for the texture these subjects demand
Prompt 3

Valley Fog at the Equinox

Subject: A river valley filling with blue-gray fog while ridge tops catch gold — the planet tilting, the light retreating into the lowlands.

The Valley Sink mix (Indigo 2:Buff Titanium 1) creates the milky-blue fog that fills lowlands as autumn deepens. Quinacridone Gold on the ridge tops catches the last equinox sun.

  • Buff Titanium’s opacity mixed with Indigo creates milky-blue fog
  • Leave ridge tops as untouched paper or dry Quinacridone Gold
  • Apply fog wash in a single pass — do not rework
  • Cobalt Blue for sky above the fog line
Prompt 4

Maple Quilt Patches

Subject: A hillside where gold maple patches appear among dark conifers — a quilt being stitched one patch at a time, autumn arriving piecemeal.

Quinacridone Gold glazed over a dry conifer background creates the patchy, quilt-like distribution of early autumn color against the unchanging green.

  • Paint the conifer background first with Hooker’s Green and let it dry completely
  • Glaze Quinacridone Gold patches over the dry background — irregularly shaped, scattered, not yet a statement
  • A few Pyrrol Scarlet accents for vine maple among the gold
  • Buff Titanium for mist threading between the patches
Notes: All pigments confirmed Daniel Smith. Green Apatite Genuine is from the PrimaTek line — genuine mineral, expect heavy granulation and settling. Transparent Red Oxide dries darker than it looks wet. Buff Titanium used for fog effects should be applied in a single pass.
