Chanterelle Gold

The forest floor pays out in gold — chanterelles under every second Douglas fir, vine maple peak scarlet screaming against black-green conifers. Morning valley fog holds until ten, coho staging in estuaries. Autumn is a spender, burning through the color budget in two weeks.

Primary Trio

New Gamboge

Chanterelle gold in duff, warm and clean; the cleanest, most transparent warm yellow available for the forest floor’s golden payout

Alizarin Crimson

Vine maple at peak against dark conifer; chosen over Pyrrol Scarlet for its transparency and blue undertone

Green Apatite Genuine

Old-growth moss textured and deep; a PrimaTek mineral pigment that granulates aggressively on cold-press

Mixes & Ratios

Fog Break

3 Buff Titanium : 1 Cobalt Blue

Valley fog thinning to reveal blue overhead — the fog is still mostly winning, but the sky is pushing through.

Buff Titanium
Cobalt Blue
Buff Titanium dominates because the fog is still mostly winning. Cobalt Blue granulates into the mix. Apply wet, starting with pure Buff Titanium at the bottom and increasing Cobalt Blue toward the top.

Chanterelle Duff

2 New Gamboge : 1 Raw Umber

Gold cap half-buried in fir needles — the chanterelle sits on the duff, distinct from it, a coin of gold on a brown floor.

New Gamboge
Raw Umber
Paint the duff (Raw Umber) first and let it dry. Then lay New Gamboge into the mushroom shape while the umber is still visible around it. The gold should not mix with the umber — the chanterelle sits on the duff, distinct from it.

Vine Blaze

2 Alizarin Crimson : 1 New Gamboge

Scarlet leaf backlit by morning sun — the gold veins showing through the crimson like light through stained glass.

Alizarin Crimson
New Gamboge
Paint the leaf shape with New Gamboge first as a thin wash. While still damp, feed Alizarin Crimson from the outer margins inward, leaving the central vein and major lateral veins as untouched gold. Alizarin is a stainer; it will not lift once dry.

Why These Colors?

Peak autumn demands maximum chromatic range in the warm spectrum. New Gamboge is the cleanest, most transparent warm yellow available. Alizarin Crimson was chosen over Pyrrol Scarlet for its transparency and blue undertone. Green Apatite Genuine and Perylene Green provide the two-depth conifer backdrop. Ivory Black anchors the deepest values. Buff Titanium handles the morning fog.

Painting Prompts

Stuck staring at a blank page? These subjects are tailor-made for this week’s palette — each one leans into the colors you already have mixed and the PNW moment happening right outside.

Prompt 1

Chanterelle Hunt: Gold in the Duff

Subject: A forager’s-eye view of chanterelles half-buried in fir needle duff — gold coins scattered on a brown floor, the forest paying out.

The Chanterelle Duff mix (New Gamboge 2:Raw Umber 1) keeps the gold distinct from the earth it sits on — the chanterelle is not the duff, it’s the reward for looking.

  • Paint the Raw Umber duff first and let it dry completely
  • Clean edges on the cap, fuzzy where the stem disappears into the ground
  • Perylene Green for deep conifer shadow at the edges of the composition
  • Vary New Gamboge concentration — some caps are deeper gold than others
Prompt 2

Vine Maple Blaze Against Conifers

Subject: Vine maple at peak scarlet backlit by morning sun, screaming against the black-green conifer wall behind it.

The Vine Blaze mix (Alizarin Crimson 2:New Gamboge 1) feeds crimson over gold to create the stained-glass translucency of a backlit autumn leaf.

  • Paint each leaf with New Gamboge first as a thin wash
  • Leave the central vein as untouched gold when feeding Alizarin Crimson from the margins
  • Perylene Green for the background conifer wall — dark enough without black
  • Ivory Black in the deepest shadow pockets where even Perylene Green isn’t enough
Prompt 3

Valley Fog Lifting at Ten

Subject: That specific October morning when valley fog thins and blue sky appears — the daily negotiation between fog and sun, fog losing by ten.

The Fog Break mix (Buff Titanium 3:Cobalt Blue 1) captures the milky, granulating transition from fog to sky — Buff Titanium dominant below, Cobalt Blue winning above.

  • Apply wet, Buff Titanium at the bottom grading to Cobalt Blue at the top
  • Leave the very top as pure Cobalt Blue — the sky has won there
  • Trees emerging from fog: trunks fading to nothing at their base
  • Quinacridone Gold for second-growth maples catching the first light above the fog line
Prompt 4

Coho Staging in the Estuary

Subject: Silver coho holding in brackish water, dark torpedo shapes in the shallows, waiting for the river to rise.

Ivory Black creates the dark silhouettes of staging coho. Perylene Green over the water area gives the brackish, tannin-stained estuary its character.

  • Coho are dark torpedo shapes — paint as silhouettes in Ivory Black, no detail needed
  • Perylene Green wash over the water area first, then drop fish shapes in while damp
  • Burnt Sienna along the banks for alder leaf litter
  • Green Apatite Genuine at the waterline for estuary silt and its characteristic granulation
Notes: All pigments confirmed Daniel Smith. Alizarin Crimson in Daniel Smith’s formulation is Quinacridone-based (PR177), not the fugitive traditional Alizarin — it’s lightfast. New Gamboge is similarly a modern reformulation with excellent permanence. Ivory Black is the warmest black in the Daniel Smith line. Green Apatite Genuine will granulate aggressively on cold-press.
