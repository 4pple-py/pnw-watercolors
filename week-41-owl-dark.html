Owl Dark

4:30 PM and the barred owl’s eight-note call drops through absolute dark. Chum salmon thrash in shin-deep creeks, their backs breaking the surface like dull knives. The forest goes silent except for death and hunger, and even the rain sounds like it’s trying not to be heard.

Mixes & Ratios

Chum Rot

2 Burnt Umber : 1 Perylene Green

Salmon carcass feeding the creekbank — the brown of death becoming the green of next year’s growth.

Burnt Umber
Perylene Green
Mix produces a deep, decayed olive-brown. Apply wet-in-wet and let the Perylene Green’s staining power pull the mix toward black at the edges. This is not a color you correct — let it settle ugly.

Barred Wing

2 Payne’s Gray : 1 Chinese White : touch Raw Sienna

Owl plumage dissolving into bark — neither bird nor tree, just pattern disappearing into pattern.

Payne's Gray
Chinese White
Raw Sienna
The Chinese White lifts the Payne’s Gray to a feathered mid-tone. The Raw Sienna touch warms it. Apply in horizontal bars with a flat brush, leaving gaps for white paper beneath.

Tannic Current

1 Raw Sienna : 1 Cascade Green : touch Payne’s Gray

Tea-dark water over pale gravel — transparent, amber-green, hiding everything and revealing everything.

Raw Sienna
Cascade Green
Payne's Gray
Transparent amber-green. The Payne’s Gray touch drops the value for depth. Glaze over dry paper for clear-water effect; wet-in-wet for turbulent stretches.

Why These Colors?

This is a palette built for painting in the dark. Payne’s Gray and Indigo provide two different blacks (blue-leaning and warm-leaning). Perylene Green is the critical hinge: dark enough to function as a near-black, green enough to remind you that living things persist. Moonglow’s tripartite granulation makes fog genuinely atmospheric. Chinese White is used sparingly, for the owl and for light on water, because this palette is about darkness earning its keep.

Painting Prompts

Darkness is not absence — it’s a subject. These prompts lean into the 4:30 PM world where everything is shape and sound, and color is something you have to earn.

Prompt 1

Barred Owl on Mossy Snag

Subject: A barred owl perched on a moss-draped snag at dusk, plumage dissolving into bark — camouflage so effective it becomes a meditation on what counts as visible.

The Barred Wing mix (Payne’s Gray 2:Chinese White 1:Raw Sienna touch) was built for owl plumage that refuses to separate from its background.

  • Paint dark background first: wet wash of Perylene Green and Indigo
  • Let dry, paint owl using Barred Wing mix in horizontal bars
  • Use Chinese White sparingly for facial disc
  • Resist adding detail to background
Prompt 2

Chum Salmon Run at Dusk

Subject: Chum salmon in shin-deep water, one spent carcass on the bank — the living and the dead in the same frame, which is what November actually looks like.

The Chum Rot mix (Burnt Umber 2:Perylene Green 1) handles the carcass. Quinacridone Burnt Orange provides the tiny, almost-hopeful punctuation of salmon eggs.

  • Start with water glazes
  • Paint living salmon as dark shapes breaking surface
  • Carcass gets full Chum Rot treatment
  • Drop Quinacridone Burnt Orange in tiny dots for eggs
Prompt 3

Creek Stones Through Dark Water

Subject: Looking down into shallow creek, pale gravel through tea-colored water — a study in transparency where the medium and the subject are the same thing.

The Tannic Current mix (Raw Sienna 1:Cascade Green 1:Payne’s Gray touch) is the water itself — glaze it over dry-painted stones and watch depth appear.

  • Paint creek stones first on dry paper
  • Let dry, glaze with thin Tannic Current wash
  • Drop Payne’s Gray into deeper spots
  • Add leaf shapes with Burnt Umber
Prompt 4

Fog Between Old-Growth Trunks

Subject: Massive old-growth trunks receding into fog at 4:30 PM — depth without detail, where the fog is more solid than the trees.

Moonglow’s tripartite granulation was made for this — it separates on cold-press into component violets, blues, and ochres that read as atmospheric depth.

  • Wet paper, lay graded Moonglow wash
  • While damp, charge trunk positions with Indigo and Payne’s Gray
  • Second layer of Payne’s Gray on nearest trunk only
  • No detail on ground plane
Notes: All pigments are Daniel Smith extra fine watercolors. Transparent Brown Oxide glazes beautifully for creek-bed depth. Moonglow separates beautifully on cold-press. Cascade Green is a Daniel Smith exclusive with a cool, slightly turquoise lean.
