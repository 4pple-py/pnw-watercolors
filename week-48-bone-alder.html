Week 48 — PNW Watercolor Series

Bone Alder

A brief clearing between atmospheric rivers. Morning frost silvers sword fern fronds into fossils. Bare alder trunks stand white as bone against charcoal sky, and wintering ducks raft in loose thousands on the Sound. Clarity so brief it feels like trespass.

Primary Trio

Ivory Black

Sky behind bare alder, charcoal wash; the high-contrast partner that makes bone-white trunks legible

Cobalt Teal Blue

Winter Sound, cold and open; semi-opaque and granulating, the cold signature of December water

Raw Umber

Frozen mud, boot-printed trail; grounding the skeleton landscape in earth

Mixes & Ratios

Frost Fern

1 Olive Green : 1 Titanium White : touch Cerulean Blue Chromium

Sword fern rimed and still — Titanium White frosts the Olive Green into muted icy sage.

Olive Green
Titanium White
Cerulean Blue Cr
Titanium White frosts the Olive Green into muted icy sage. Cerulean Blue Chromium adds mineral-cold edge. Dry-brush on rough paper — white catches peaks while green sinks into valleys.

Alder Bone

2 Titanium White : touch Raw Umber : touch Ivory Black

Bare trunk against storm sky — alder bark is genuinely, startlingly white.

Titanium White
Raw Umber
Ivory Black
Start with Titanium White applied thickly. Touch Raw Umber to shadow side and Ivory Black to base. The white should dominate — alder bark is genuinely, startlingly white.

Duck Raft

2 Cobalt Teal Blue : 1 Indanthrone Blue : touch Raw Umber

Hundreds of birds on gray-green water — paint the raft as broken shape, not individual birds.

Cobalt Teal Blue
Indanthrone Blue
Raw Umber
Cobalt Teal Blue provides cold open-water color. Indanthrone Blue deepens where raft clusters. Raw Umber muddies just enough for living bodies displacing water. Paint raft as broken shape, not individual birds.

Why These Colors?

This palette is built on the brief clearing — that two-hour window when the landscape reveals its skeleton. Ivory Black and Titanium White are the high-contrast pair that makes alder trunks legible. Cobalt Teal Blue is the Sound’s cold signature. Potter’s Pink captures the rose-colored clearing light. Lunar Black’s extreme granulation gives retreating storm clouds geological texture.

Painting Prompts

Stuck staring at a blank page? These subjects are tailor-made for this week’s palette — each one leans into the colors you already have mixed and the PNW moment happening right outside.

Prompt 1

Bone Alders Against Storm Sky

Subject: Row of bare white alder trunks against charcoal sky — skeleton trees revealed in the brief clearing.

Ivory Black and Titanium White are the high-contrast pair built for this moment. Lunar Black’s granulation gives the retreating storm geological texture.

  • Paint sky with thinned Ivory Black leaving trunk spaces
  • Drop Lunar Black into retreating storm for granulating cloud texture
  • Apply Alder Bone mix thickly
  • Leave light-facing side as pure Titanium White
Prompt 2

Duck Raft on the Winter Sound

Subject: Hundreds of ducks rafted on cold water, seen from shore — a broken irregular dark shape, not individual birds.

The Duck Raft mix (Cobalt Teal Blue 2:Indanthrone Blue 1:Raw Umber touch) handles both water and birds as one merged shape.

  • Flat wash of Cobalt Teal Blue for water
  • Drop Duck Raft mix where birds cluster
  • Paint raft as broken irregular dark shape NOT individual birds
  • Potter’s Pink strip along distant horizon
Prompt 3

Frost Ferns at Dawn

Subject: Sword fern fronds rimed with frost, dark understory behind — morning frost silvering fronds into fossils.

The Frost Fern mix (Olive Green 1:Titanium White 1:Cerulean Blue Chromium touch) dry-brushed on rough paper catches frost on peaks.

  • Paint dark background with Ivory Black and Indanthrone Blue
  • Apply Olive Green to frond shapes
  • Once dry, dry-brush Frost Fern mix on rough paper
  • Touch Cerulean Blue Chromium to frost edges
Prompt 4

The Brief Clearing — Alder Grove with Rose Sky

Subject: Wider landscape with bone-white trunks, frozen trail, rose sky — clarity so brief it feels like trespass.

Potter’s Pink carries the clearing sky. Alder Bone mix for trunks. Raw Umber grounds the frozen trail. Burnt Sienna Light warms the nearest catkins.

  • Potter’s Pink sky in clearing zone to Lunar Black storm
  • Alder trunks with Alder Bone mix
  • Trail is Raw Umber with Ivory Black boot-prints
  • Burnt Sienna Light catkins on nearest branches
Notes: All pigments are Daniel Smith extra fine watercolors. Lunar Black is a PrimaTek pigment with dramatic granulation. Cobalt Teal Blue is semi-opaque and granulates moderately. Potter’s Pink (PR233) is subtle, low-chroma, granulates gently.
