Week 49 — PNW Watercolor Series

Year’s Last Breath

December 29 through 31. The year’s final light falls on eagles still posted along rivers, on saturated forest floor where skunk cabbage buds gather force underground. Everything is gray and soaked and quietly, enormously alive. Ending that is already beginning — the forest knows what we haven’t noticed yet, and the skunk cabbage is proof.

Perylene Green

Shimmer of the turn, light shifting between pink and gold; the visual equivalent of the year turning

Mixes & Ratios

Underground Promise

1 Cascade Green : 1 Perylene Green : touch Quinacridone Gold

Skunk cabbage bud forming in dark soil — two greens layered, cool brightness against blackish depth.

Cascade Green
Perylene Green
Quin. Gold
Two greens layered — Cascade Green’s cool brightness against Perylene Green’s blackish depth. Apply Perylene Green first as dark soil, let dry, glaze Cascade Green into bud shape. Touch Gold into center while wet.

Year Turn

1 Duochrome Cactus Flower : 2 Moonglow

Iridescent shift where the old year thins — Duochrome Cactus Flower shifts between pink and gold depending on viewing angle.

Duochrome CF
Moonglow
Duochrome Cactus Flower shifts between pink and gold depending on viewing angle. Apply to damp paper and let both pigments do their separation work undisturbed. Do not overwork.

Neutral Tint and Indigo produce a layered dark that shifts between blue-black and true neutral. Buff Titanium touch catches where last light hits river surface. Eagle is dark shape against slightly less dark sky.

Why These Colors?

This is the year’s final palette, and it earns the right to include a duochrome. Duochrome Cactus Flower is the visual equivalent of the year turning. Moonglow provides the granulating sky. Perylene Green is at its most honest here: the forest floor in late December is the greenest it will ever be. Cascade Green carries the future: the skunk cabbage bud generating metabolic heat underground.

Painting Prompts

Stuck staring at a blank page? These subjects are tailor-made for this week’s palette — each one leans into the colors you already have mixed and the PNW moment happening right outside.

Prompt 1

Skunk Cabbage Bud Emerging

Subject: Close-up of dark saturated forest floor with single skunk cabbage bud — the forest knows what we haven’t noticed yet.

The Underground Promise mix (Cascade Green 1:Perylene Green 1:Quinacridone Gold touch) layers cool brightness against blackish depth — the bud generating metabolic heat underground.

  • Paint entire surface with Perylene Green first, wet and saturated
  • Drop Raw Umber into lower areas while wet
  • Once dry, glaze Cascade Green into bud shape
  • Touch Quinacridone Gold into bud center while damp
Prompt 2

Eagle Vigil on the River

Subject: Eagle silhouetted on bare cottonwood above winter river at 4:15 PM — still posted, still watching.

The Eagle Vigil mix (Neutral Tint 2:Indigo 1:Buff Titanium touch) produces a layered dark for silhouette. Single dot of Quinacridone Gold for the eagle’s eye.

  • Paint sky as graded Neutral Tint and Indigo wash
  • Eagle and tree as one dark silhouette
  • Buff Titanium thin horizontal line for last light on river
  • Single dot of Quinacridone Gold for eagle’s eye
Prompt 3

Year Turn — Duochrome Sky Study

Subject: Abstract sky study for year’s last evening — the shimmer of the turn, light shifting between pink and gold.

The Year Turn mix (Duochrome Cactus Flower 1:Moonglow 2) shimmers over darks, invisible over white — apply last, as a final glaze.

  • Soak paper and lay Moonglow generously
  • Charge Indigo and Neutral Tint into darkest areas
  • Let dry COMPLETELY
  • Glaze Duochrome Cactus Flower over dark areas — it shimmers over darks, invisible over white
Prompt 4

Rose Hip and Wet Bark

Subject: Single remaining rose hip clinging to bare cane against saturated backdrop — one warm point in a green-gray world.

Transparent Pyrrol Orange at full concentration, sharp edge, on dry paper — the emotional center of a painting built on negative space.

  • Paint background first: Perylene Green, Raw Umber, Neutral Tint merged wet
  • Leave negative space for hip and cane
  • Cane is thinned Raw Umber with tiny thorn flicks
  • Rose hip gets pure Transparent Pyrrol Orange at full concentration, sharp edge, on dry paper
Prompt 5

Storm Watching from the Coast

Subject: King tides and winter storm surf from a bluff — the year ending in spray and force.

Moonglow wet sky charged with Neutral Tint storm clouds. Indigo ocean at full strength. Buff Titanium flicked for foam.

  • Block sky with Moonglow wet, charge Neutral Tint into storm clouds
  • Ocean is Indigo at full strength
  • Spray and foam are Buff Titanium flicked from loaded brush
  • Eagle is tiny dark shape high in sky
Notes: All pigments are Daniel Smith extra fine watercolors. Duochrome Cactus Flower is an interference pigment that shows best over dark values — over white paper it’s nearly invisible. Cascade Green is a Daniel Smith convenience mix with a cool, slightly turquoise character. Apply the duochrome last, as a final glaze, for maximum effect.
