Mixes & Ratios

Fog Held

2 Moonglow : 1 Carbazole Violet : touch Buff Titanium

Valley fog refusing to lift — Moonglow separates into violet, blue, and pink while Carbazole Violet pushes toward darker end.

Moonglow
Carbazole Violet
Buff Titanium
Moonglow separates into violet, blue, and pink. Carbazole Violet pushes toward darker end. Buff Titanium prevents full opacity. Apply very wet on cold-press and do NOT tilt — freezing fog doesn’t move.

King Tide

2 French Ultramarine : 1 Phthalo Green BS

Salt water where freshwater belongs — French Ultramarine’s granulation meets Phthalo Green’s staining intensity.

French Ultramarine
Phthalo Green BS
French Ultramarine’s granulation meets Phthalo Green’s staining intensity. Apply French Ultramarine first, charge Phthalo Green from edges, let it creep inward like tide.

Wren Song

1 Transparent Red Oxide : 1 Burnt Sienna : touch Raw Umber

Tiny warm body, enormous voice — two iron oxides blending seamlessly, Raw Umber darkening back and tail.

Trans. Red Oxide
Burnt Sienna
Raw Umber
Two iron oxides blend seamlessly. Raw Umber touch darkens back and tail. Paint the wren small — it should almost disappear. The song is the subject, not the bird.

Why These Colors?

Carbazole Violet is the palette’s philosophical center — a staining, transparent violet so dark it reads as the year’s nadir. French Ultramarine handles the king tide with gravitas. Burnt Sienna and Transparent Red Oxide provide the warm earth range. Moonglow is the fog engine. Lamp Black is the blunt instrument. Phthalo Green BS is the salt-water stain that doesn’t belong in the marsh.

Painting Prompts

Stuck staring at a blank page? These subjects are tailor-made for this week’s palette — each one leans into the colors you already have mixed and the PNW moment happening right outside.

Prompt 1

Freezing Fog in the Valley

Subject: Valley at 8 AM, fog so thick it’s swallowed trees beyond fifty feet — breath held too long, refusing to lift.

The Fog Held mix (Moonglow 2:Carbazole Violet 1:Buff Titanium touch) was built for fog that doesn’t move — apply on flat paper and let it sit.

  • Wet paper and apply Fog Held mix, heavier in distance — do NOT tilt
  • Let dry, paint fence posts with Raw Umber, decreasing in contrast
  • Apply Cerulean Blue Chromium dry brush for frost
  • Add Buff Titanium to frost highlights
Prompt 2

King Tide Flooding the Marsh

Subject: Coastal marsh at high king tide, salt water creeping through grass — rearranging what belongs where.

The King Tide mix (French Ultramarine 2:Phthalo Green BS 1) creeps inward like the tide itself. Phthalo Green stains permanently — use deliberately.

  • Paint marsh grass first with Burnt Sienna, leaving gaps
  • Apply French Ultramarine into gaps, charge Phthalo Green from edges
  • Phthalo Green stains permanently — use deliberately
  • Glaze Moonglow for distant fog, Lamp Black for 4 PM sky
Prompt 3

Pacific Wren on Frost-Covered Stump

Subject: Tiny wren beak open mid-song against cold, dark, violet world — the only voice, insisting.

The Wren Song mix (Transparent Red Oxide 1:Burnt Sienna 1:Raw Umber touch) paints the bird small. The song is the subject, not the bird.

  • Paint background first: Carbazole Violet wet and dark
  • Leave negative space for bird and stump
  • Stump is Raw Umber with Buff Titanium frost
  • Wren gets Wren Song mix, paint SMALL, no bigger than thumbnail
Prompt 4

Solstice Sword Ferns in Frost

Subject: Close-up of sword fern fronds coated in frost crystals — dark understory, mineral-cold edges.

Cerulean Blue Chromium is genuinely opaque, granulates heavily — ideal for frost on Phthalo Green fronds against a Carbazole Violet dark.

  • Block in dark background with Carbazole Violet and Lamp Black
  • Paint fronds with Phthalo Green BS
  • Dry-brush Cerulean Blue Chromium along pinnae edges for frost
  • Touch Buff Titanium to tips where frost is thickest
Notes: All pigments are Daniel Smith extra fine watercolors. Carbazole Violet (PV23) is one of the most staining pigments. Moonglow is a convenience mix of Ultramarine Blue, Viridian, and Alizarin Crimson analog. Cerulean Blue Chromium is genuinely opaque, granulates heavily — ideal for frost.
