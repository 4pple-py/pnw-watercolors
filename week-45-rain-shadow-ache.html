Rain Shadow Ache

Sequim sits in pale winter sun while Seattle drowns under its ninth straight day. Anna’s hummingbird fluffs to twice its size on a frozen feeder at dawn, 7:40 AM, the sky a furious pink it doesn’t deserve. The tension: longing for light twenty miles east, unreachable — the Olympics between you and the cure.

Mixes & Ratios

Torpor Dawn

1 Opera Pink : 2 Buff Titanium : touch Aussie Red Gold

Hummingbird surviving sunrise — frosty dawn glow with one warm edge for gorget.

Opera Pink
Buff Titanium
Aussie Red Gold
Buff Titanium tames Opera Pink’s fluorescence to a frosty dawn glow. Aussie Red Gold touch warms one edge for gorget. Apply as graded wash, warmer at horizon. Leave hard-edged negative shape for bird.

Shadow Curtain

2 Moonglow : 1 Indanthrone Blue

Indanthrone Blue
Moonglow’s tripartite granulation makes this feel like actual weather. Indanthrone Blue deepens without killing separation. Apply very wet on tilted paper and let gravity do the rain.

Why These Colors?

This palette is split down the middle, like the rain shadow itself. The cool side carries Seattle’s perpetual dusk. The warm side carries the stolen sunrise. Moonglow bridges them. Opera Pink is technically fugitive — the right metaphor for a sunrise that lasts twelve minutes. Aussie Red Gold is a Daniel Smith exclusive that captures the iridescent gorget flash no other pigment can.

Painting Prompts

Stuck staring at a blank page? These subjects are tailor-made for this week’s palette — each one leans into the colors you already have mixed and the PNW moment happening right outside.

Prompt 1

Anna’s Hummingbird at Frozen Feeder

Subject: Hummingbird fluffed to twice its size, perched on frost-covered feeder at 7:40 AM. The sky behind is a furious pink it doesn’t deserve.

The Torpor Dawn mix (Opera Pink 1:Buff Titanium 2:Aussie Red Gold touch) was built for this exact moment — frosty dawn glow with one warm edge for gorget.

  • Paint sky first as graded wash: Opera Pink at horizon to Buff Titanium above, leave negative shape for bird
  • Paint feeder with Buff Titanium + Payne’s Gray, white paper for frost
  • Bird is Payne’s Gray body with sharp edges; touch Aussie Red Gold to gorget while damp
  • Gorget is emotional center — use Aussie Red Gold at full concentration
Prompt 2

Rain Shadow Divide from Hurricane Ridge

Subject: Looking west, Sequim in sun on one side, rain curtain on Seattle side — the Olympics splitting two different weathers.

The Shadow Curtain mix (Moonglow 2:Indanthrone Blue 1) handles the rain wall, while Phthalo Blue RS carries the cold clear sky on the Sequim side.

  • Divide sky in half: Phthalo Blue RS for Sequim side, Shadow Curtain mix for Seattle side
  • Paint Olympic ridge with Payne’s Gray
  • Glaze Potter’s Pink onto snowfields
  • Rain curtain: tilt paper and let Moonglow-Indanthrone mix run vertically
Prompt 3

Winter King Bolete in Frost

Subject: Close-up of bolete cap cracked by cold, frost in fissures — earthen and quiet.

The Bolete Cap mix (Raw Umber 2:Payne’s Gray 1) produces cracked-earth texture when applied thick and layered.

  • Paint cap with Bolete Cap mix applied thick on dry paper
  • Once dry, use Buff Titanium in cracks for pale flesh
  • Glaze second Raw Umber layer into deepest crevices
  • Dry-brush Buff Titanium along cap edges for frost
Prompt 4

Twelve-Minute Sunrise

Subject: Pure sky study — stolen Sequim sunrise, twelve minutes of furious pink before gray reclaims everything.

Opera Pink at full strength, charged with Quinacridone Rose where it cools — Moonglow takes over the upper sky as the pink fades.

  • Soak paper and work fast
  • Drop Opera Pink at horizon, charge Quinacridone Rose where pink cools
  • Let Moonglow take over upper sky
  • Mountain silhouette is Payne’s Gray and Indanthrone Blue while sky still damp
Notes: All pigments are Daniel Smith extra fine watercolors. Opera Pink (PR122:BV10) is lightfast-rated only fair. Aussie Red Gold is a Daniel Smith exclusive with genuine color-shift properties. Phthalo Blue (Red Shade) is warmer, less green than standard. Quinacridone Rose provides a lightfast alternative to Opera Pink.
